Goal:
Implement the mixed-surface settlement shell so concealed civic infrastructure, covert access layers, and hidden understructures can coexist beneath ordinary habitation, trade, and governance surfaces without splitting into unrelated town and wilderness packets.

Scope:

* support civic-hazard hybrid settlements where ordinary movement, trade, and habitation coexist with trap pressure, active predator ecology, or unstable soft-hostility zones under one coherent settlement surface
* support protected enclave and refuge-containment duality so some districts simultaneously function as sanctuary, surveillance zone, social experiment, and soft containment — lawful hospitality slides from refuge into monitored assimilation without open imprisonment
* support victim-guide epistemics: victim-guides provide core operational facts through trauma, obsession, or corruption while distorting motives, names, or causal models; guide usefulness and guide truthfulness remain separate variables rather than one binary trust state
* support anomaly ally and false suspect overlap: a true anomaly ally may present as a valid false suspect whose body size, diet, solitude, nocturnal routine, and origin produce genuinely incriminating patterns even while helping against the main threat; eliminating the wrong entity creates a false-clean ending that suppresses symptoms briefly while preserving the real hostile pattern; injury thresholds may force involuntary revelation turning the ally into a short-run hazard
* support support-NPC betrayal and selective aid: support actors may remain emotionally attached but not fully emancipated with rescue-imprint bonds, nightly origin returns, and curiosity-driven probing; selective aid allows a credible helper to offer real healing or knowledge while withholding the one intervention that would break the antagonist's plan; helper-role antagonists may fake death through immunities or staged body states; faction allies should not default to gratitude and may exploit investigators, discard them when convenient, or turn against them after the shared objective is met
* support hidden anomaly spaces authored in two passes: civic or travel function first, hostile or ritual underlayer second; urban tunnels, false tombs, crypts behind false walls, and owner-biased trap geometry where the resident bypasses the defense while intruders absorb it
* support settlement architecture that adapts to threat ecology: elevated paths, stilts, suspended links, nightly routines, or restricted movement zones when persistent predators or instability shape everyday life; hidden undercity or parasitic value-seeking threats coexisting beneath a settlement defined by one dominant craft without replacing the surface identity
* support city-originating jobs that terminate at concealed offsite locations such as underwater buildings or hidden river structures; hidden access telegraphed through sensory clues such as drafts, smell shifts, and candle behavior rather than overt search declarations
* support public sacred and civic sites reversing into coercive hidden-depth scenes: temples, manors, sealed rooms, crypt routes, and controlled-capture infrastructure; present-day settlement activity sitting over buried objectives forcing retrieval through active civic surfaces, monuments, fountains, and public traffic
* support village-bound wilderness stewards whose authority and obligations derive from local community rather than distant hierarchy; low-technology ritual operators as socially embedded civic figures
* support monsters and nonhumans as special-entry content introduced through import, escape, patron commission, distant lands, hidden communities, or singular encounters rather than ambient background density; legal status, cultural assimilation, and visible ancestry remain separable identity surfaces
* support ordinary civic and historical sites such as mills, roadsides, keeps, vats, and workshops as valid mission anchors once one strong complication is attached; adventure seeding does not depend on overtly anomalous ruins or dungeon spaces only
* support strongholds as civic clusters — fortress, court, prison, market anchor, school, religious center, and storage hub rather than pure combat maps; specialist-archetype overlays such as wizard keeps, cathedral-core priest fortresses, ranger forts, druid sites, dwarf citadels, elven sanctuaries, halfling holds, and orc forts change labor policy, terrain use, symbolic center, and geometry without replacing underlying site grammar; reusable exemplar castle packets shipped as concrete site templates
* support wrecks and derelicts with explicit integrity grades communicating access, collapse risk, salvage promise, and current usefulness; derelicts may become long-lived habitat shells whose current residents are ecologically unrelated to the original crew; isolated salvage-lords may repurpose local dead into labor and defense
* support captives hidden as inert environment objects: petrified, transformed, or otherwise inert-looking objects inside ordinary rooms, storage, or structural features rather than obvious cells; some detainees require body-state suppression because ordinary cages fail against alternate-form movement
* support civilian-venue abduction pipelines where inns, markets, or comparable public spaces conceal multi-step capture, transfer, cage, or feeding pipelines; urban encounters driven by law, curfew, checkpoints, registries, bureaucracy, and guild politics rather than wilderness threat alone
* support detention facility dual control where formal procedures and overseers govern the upper zones while deeper zones develop inmate rulers, contraband hierarchies, monster residents, and decayed practical authority
* keep this parent as the shared boundary and completion shell; route concealed regional habitation and low-footprint surface presentation to SPE-805; route unified undercity circulation and mixed-faction hidden-route networks to SPE-806; route institution-specific hidden depth and restricted lower-level access to SPE-451

Constraints:

* no ambient monster density as a default settlement assumption
* no overtly anomalous site framing required — ordinary civic spaces are valid anchors
* settlement surface identity must remain coherent even when underlayer is hostile or covert
* prefer layered site authoring (civic function first, concealed function second) over standalone dungeon logic
* reuse existing site, routing, and hidden-state infrastructure where possible

Acceptance criteria:

* at least one settlement site carries a covert underlayer that can be reached through ordinary civic surface movement rather than a separate dungeon entrance
* at least one victim-guide interaction tracks guide usefulness and guide truthfulness as independent variables
* at least one anomaly ally interaction can produce a false-clean ending if the ally is misidentified as the primary threat
* at least one civilian venue conceals an abduction or containment pipeline reachable through urban bureaucratic pressure rather than combat
* at least one stronghold or civic cluster uses a specialist-archetype overlay that changes its labor policy and symbolic center without replacing the underlying site grammar
* targeted tests or authoring examples cover the two-pass authoring model, sensory-cue access telegraphing, and owner-biased trap geometry

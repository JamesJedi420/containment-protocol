Goal:
Implement staged parasitic host conversion so implanted anomaly cores rewrite hosts through visible, deterministic progression instead of one-step curse logic.

Scope:

* support implanted or attached anomaly cores that progressively rewrite a host body and capability profile
* support neurochemical overwrite so some parasite classes drive aggression, panic, or homicidal impulse through host brain chemistry rather than direct consciousness displacement
* support neural lodging in nonobvious anchor sites such as deep-brain positions rather than only surface, spine, or chest cavities
* support visible live-body cues such as subdermal movement and migrating surface changes as early detection signals before full confirmation
* support multiple distinct ingress paths including direct implantation, voluntary summoning, host resonance, and moral-rewrite entry rather than assuming one generic infection model
* support self-reproducing parasite classes where one surviving specimen can hide inside the last carrier and restart the cycle, making containment of even minimal populations operationally critical
* support explicit extraction lethality where forced removal kills or severely damages the host rather than always being a safe treatment option
* support co-infection antagonism where two simultaneous parasite classes may destroy each other rather than stacking linearly
* let the process advance through visible stages before full conversion
* support delayed attribute drain, cure windows, and later loss of control or actor replacement
* connect progression to body-plan reveal, cognition loss, new actor emergence, transformation, possession, and hidden-state systems
* preserve inspectable stage state suitable for intervention and long-horizon consequence handling

Constraints:

* deterministic only
* no instant-transform default for all parasitic effects
* no need for full biological simulation
* no assumption that extraction is always a safe treatment option
* no assumption that additional infestation always escalates in one linear direction
* prefer compact staged lifecycle rules over sprawling infection trees
* keep cure timing, stage progression, ingress path, and extraction risk legible enough for players to act on them

Acceptance criteria:

* at least one host conversion advances through more than one stage
* at least one stage changes the host before full replacement
* at least one cure window matters before the hard end state
* at least one parasite operates through neurochemical overwrite rather than direct possession theater
* at least one parasite occupies a nonobvious neural or deep-tissue anchor site rather than a surface cavity
* at least one self-reproducing parasite class makes single-specimen containment operationally critical
* at least one forced extraction damages or kills the host rather than succeeding safely
* targeted tests cover deterministic staged host conversion, drain progression, ingress path variation, neurochemical overwrite, extraction risk, and cure timing

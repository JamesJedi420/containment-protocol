Goal:
Implement the template authoring kernel so new scenario, hazard, doctrine, and support content can be added through reusable template shapes rather than bespoke per-case logic.

Scope:

* define a stable template kernel for requirements, conditions, modifiers, risk hooks, investigation visibility, reward-family hooks, and presentation metadata
* support at least three reusable content-family templates such as protocol materials, hazards/incidents, and doctrine or playbook assets
* support bounded contextual variation, investigation-first and preparation-first authoring, and deterministic "usually X unless Y" behavior
* support scenario packet authoring for geometry, routes, mission wrappers, objective structure, reward finishing, and bounded procedural variation without freeform scripting
* support support-asset taxonomy, depletion models, deceptive variants, identification workflow, and action/power-band validation rules
* make template boundaries explicit so authors can add or tune content without changing unrelated runtime systems
* support issue and feature brief authoring with implementation surface, expected runtime behavior, and intended player-facing result declared as separate authoring slots rather than one blended description; core premise should remain separable from the system grammar built around it so one fantasy can support several valid rule structures without forcing the first mechanical reading to dominate
* support sandbox preparation pipelines where geography and locale structure come before plotted story linkage; hooks and local plots should emerge by scanning already-authored notes and connecting existing locales rather than by inventing detached quest lines; hybrid world hooks should remain composable so two or three hook packets can be intentionally mixed into one region frame
* support settlement and kingdom packet authoring driven by first-class world hooks with climate, site type, culture, situation, and history all valid primary entry points; hook-first design should propagate downstream into polity shape, threat pressure, economic identity, and local adventure calls; region packets should remain inspiration-first but structurally complete enough for immediate use, with compact civic, geographic, and hook fields rather than exhaustive gazetteer depth
* support top-down spatial authoring workflow so authored spaces are built from region-scale structure down to settlement and building-or-interior detail rather than from isolated rooms alone; larger geography, local districting, and interior layout should remain connected so downstream routing, rumor distribution, and pressure generation inherit the right context
* support scale-critical key frames and structured visual-reference assets for content packets whose gameplay depends on scale, atmosphere, posture, or spatial reading; pose, prop, costume, and body-language references should remain reusable production support assets rather than ad hoc one-off fixes
* support compact chained-table NPC authoring where a small linked-table architecture emits geography, role, personality, and presentation outputs through well-layered table chains; compact authoring should remain a first-class strategy for rapid packet generation rather than a sign of reduced depth
* support compact one-to-four-hour ruin packets with embedded map-chain progression so clue objects, route maps, or directional artifacts point toward the next ruin and let progression emerge from recovered physical evidence; the same packet may appear as a fixed landmark or as a travel-layer discovery without changing its internal logic; economically meaningful outbuildings, fields, barns, yards, and support structures should remain valid content surfaces for hidden rewards and operational state rather than decorative filler
* support secure-site operations as survey-plan-execute loops beginning with outside survey and partial floorplan intelligence, passing through an explicit planning phase before execution under live resistance; survey should reduce uncertainty without eliminating undiscovered entries, hidden approaches, or internal unknowns entirely; tactical path diversity, layered defenses, and multi-objective complexity should remain first-class planning surfaces inside the hostile site
* support role-diverse patrol and occupancy doctrine so hostile sites derive depth from functional role diversity including cooks, jailers, guards, commanders, beasts, servants, smiths, torturers, and hidden elites occupying one facility ecology; authored patrols and room populations should communicate believable operational purpose as well as combat value
* support macro-to-micro encounter insertion aligned to passage width, approach vector, or room class; authored spaces should deliberately vary in detail density with richer interaction logic reserved for rooms with higher tactical or narrative leverage while lower-importance spaces remain concise but functional
* support encounter geometry template selection keyed to route class or site context; patrols should be authorable as structured compositions with commander, specialist, escort, and line roles rather than flat bundles
* support dual-layer power acquisition where some ability families require both broad enabling discipline investment and separate purchase of specific learned effects; parallel power families such as magic and faith-like miracles may share core resolution surfaces while still diverging in gating, acquisition, and contradiction behavior; template authoring should preserve family identity without duplicating full runtime logic for each tradition
* support procedural crypt-loot by burial class and room-alternate presentation so burial sites derive rewards from corpse count, burial class, and social tier; authored spaces may expose a recommended presentation plus bounded alternates supporting variation in tone, population, or narrative emphasis without a full rewrite
* support fit-sensitive stealth garments whose concealment value depends on correct fit, intact construction, and proper wearing state; support environment-dependent item power for gear that degrades under hostile exposure, distance, or time away from its origin while remaining operationally distinct; support nonstandard detection semantics so enhanced item states do not always appear as ordinary magical aura signatures; support charged weapons and specialty ammunition with typed secondary payloads such as sleep, blindness, vapor release, or similar subclasses through the same item-authoring grammar; support ammunition-borne payloads with independent toxin logic, shelf life, environmental spoilage, and refresh requirements
* support charged-item authoring with persistent charge state, depletion rules, and family-specific payload pools; support good-versus-bad counterpart substitution, hidden adverse state, and delayed reveal for apparently valuable items without bespoke per-item scripting; support item families that grant scan, reveal, locate, classify, or anti-concealment capabilities as payload-bearing support assets; support activation and payload legality depending on actor compatibility, role family, doctrine family, or item-source family where relevant; support bounded post-generation quality drift so similar results do not collapse into one fixed value band
* support compact persistent support-node effect families for narrow persistent effects such as regeneration, sustenance, storage, enhancement, or protection that remain compatible with depletion, positional limits, and identification rules
* support support-asset cadence limits, role-lock, and weekly-use class tools; support bundled capability platforms behind escalating rarity or qualification gates; support containment vessels and similar assets that hold hostile entities while preserving a post-release mood or hostility state based on how confinement occurred; support explicit support-asset families including recovery-class, hidden-state reveal, and attribute-enhancing artifacts
* support tutorial mission packets that teach core operational surfaces directly rather than separating rules and introductory content; support mapped hazardous-site exploration with discrete hostile entities, trap and hazard nodes, and room-or-zone-keyed interaction states as first-class mission wrappers; support both curated entity templates and bounded procedural species or variant generation from trait tables without abandoning compendium governance; support doctrine-weighted success scoring for scenarios where lawful or protocol-correct resolution carries as much weight as extraction value or kills
* support hidden-score versus public-power reward splits where some rewards remain concealed score or value objects while others become visible power commitments with active tactical meaning; reward objects themselves may be trapped, deceptive, noisy, owner-seeking, or otherwise dangerous rather than passive value piles; support active-slot limits for enhancement families so possessing more copies does not automatically stack public power
* support scenario packets with mirrored or fixed starting parties for comparative benchmarking; support multi-axis scoring combining eliminations, problem solving, treasure or asset recovery, and progression depth rather than simple win/loss; support seeded objective cards insertable into existing reward or content streams to create scenario-specific goals without bespoke map scripting
* support explicit scenario victory conditions defined as strategic goals such as hold, deny, occupy, protect, or inflict threshold damage rather than generic elimination; support scoring from control and denial of resource value, strategic nodes, or timed objectives; support multi-start scenario modes with precommitted rosters, hidden deployment, reserve uncertainty, and staged arrival schedules; support scenario authoring tightly coupled to geography, timed reinforcements, and map-keyed objectives
* support objective-chart design that makes permanent alliances strategically unstable by rewarding shifting priorities; multi-axis scoring should reward wider state management and objective fulfillment rather than single-axis extermination behavior
* support role-compatible versus incompatible asset use with explicit operator compatibility, owner-binding, or incompatibility penalties; support globally unique apex assets that may require trial-and-error identification or multi-part ordered assembly; support modular benefit-and-drawback tables where uniqueness emerges from constrained combinations; support assets that graft irreversibly to the body or alter identity; support compact weapon or relic packages that add unique verbs such as reflection, cancellation, slaying, fear, or summoning rather than only flat numeric bonuses
* support doctrine-specific organization templates across multiple echelons with nested composition from large formation to sub-formation to role package; support exemplar profile cards carrying mixed technical, physical, social, or professional ratings for scenario setup, benchmarking, and reference use
* support mapped-theme conversion guides that translate outside inspirations into internal archetype groups without copying source fiction directly; support a lore-to-mechanics translation workflow that converts narrative or lore material into mechanical templates through an explicit process rather than ad hoc stat guessing
* support context-rich procedural scenario assembly varying by political state, reserve posture, unknown-contact state, asymmetry package, objective logic, and special deployment constraints; templates should express alternate entry conditions, reserve rules, hidden-state hooks, and objective variants without bespoke scripting per case
* support rough composition-cost heuristics as compact reusable point-like weight values for team packages, threat packages, support assets, and scenario modules; these values serve authoring sanity checks and assembly guidance rather than exact balance promises or a player-facing market simulator

Constraints:

* deterministic only
* no generic data-driven-everything rewrite
* no freeform scripting layer
* no assumption that a blank template shape implies finished runtime behavior without explicit authored content
* prefer a small number of stable template shapes over many bespoke formats
* keep templates as authoring surfaces and hooks, not replacements for canonical runtime logic
* power-band and action-band discipline should remain a bounded authoring and validation rule, not a full numeric balance simulator

Acceptance criteria:

* at least three content families use explicit template-based authoring patterns
* adding a new entry in those families requires minimal or no bespoke runtime logic
* templates support shared hooks for modifiers, conditions, investigation gating, contextual variation, and rewards in a reusable way
* support-asset depletion, deception, and identification are represented through the template grammar where relevant
* validation enforces bounded action/power-band discipline in authored content
* at least one scenario uses multi-axis scoring, a strategic victory condition, or a doctrine-weighted success evaluation rather than kill-or-extract only
* at least one support asset uses cadence limits, compatibility gates, or a containment vessel with post-release state
* at least one apex asset uses modular benefit-and-drawback generation or multi-part assembly rather than hand-authored unique values
* at least one authored hostile site encodes role-diverse occupancy and variable room-detail density rather than flat encounter bundling
* at least one ruin or hazardous-site packet supports dual insertion modes and cross-site clue-chain objects
* composition-cost heuristics cover at least one scenario module or team package assembly check
* targeted tests verify template validation, contextual variation, deterministic runtime hookup, support-asset depletion, power-band compliance, and seeded-objective insertion

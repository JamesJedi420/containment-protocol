Goal:
Implement a bounded haunt-resolution layer so unresolved dead are cleared through a repeatable workflow of subdual, diagnosis, body recovery, funerary handling, and sanctified closure rather than simple defeat.

Scope:

* support haunt states that persist until one or more attachment conditions are identified and cleared, including neglected remains, desecration, unfinished rites, and death-linked anchor objects
* support a formal resolution chain including subdual or stabilization, diagnosis, anchor discovery, remains or anchor handling, sanctioned closure, and post-resolution verification
* support distributed anchor coverage where active conditions may depend on remains, linked objects, preserved fragments, or multi-part sentimental anchors rather than one corpse-only checklist; anchor surfaces include remains, murder implements, reforged material descendants, worn heirlooms, and distributed bodily fragments surviving cremation or destruction
* support multi-anchor relationship structures where a single haunting combines a living emotional router, a separate sustaining anchor object, and distributed remains carriers rather than one unified source
* distinguish temporary disruption, temporary banishment, apparent resolution, and final retirement as separate outcomes with explicit transition rules; temporary disruption through narrow repellents or sanctified loads buys time without creating false permanent resolution
* support false-clear states where a ritual appears to succeed but a residual or misclassified presence remains active until a later confirmation window or fresh trigger proves otherwise
* support recurrence after partial success when a primary anchor condition remains unresolved, including both broken containment and self-resolution pursuit paths
* support withdrawal-first and nonoccupation outcomes for hazards that are functionally non-neutralizable during their active window, treating moratorium or preventing future habitation over violated ground as valid resolution rather than mandatory source destruction
* support investigation-first and nonviolent resolution routes (truth restoration, restitution, confession, relational release, willing sacrifice, empathy, fulfilled longing) when biography and anchor state allow
* support resolution routing by origin: sudden-death confusion, unfinished-work persistence, guardian stewardship, correction-seeking justice, vengeance, curse-forged states, and pact-born immortality failures each shape motive, communication pattern, and closure path rather than collapsing into one universal exorcism
* support permanent phantom-site overlays where a ruined or absent location presents itself as preserved through emotional imprint rather than ordinary illusion, governing interaction inside the site
* support multi-part sentimental anchors requiring destruction of both a main artifact and a specific embedded personal remnant for final retirement; manifestation mode may switch by relational context, letting a spirit remain ethereal generally but become corporeal or more interactive near specific family anchors
* support body-destruction, temporary banishment, and final retirement as distinct outcomes; some entities reconstruct a vessel after a powerless window unless the true rite or anchor condition is resolved; post-destruction states may persist as noninteractive apparition, omen, or tracking residue while the entity is reforming
* support active locations as threat engines rather than passive scenery, able to conceal, trap, separate, reshape navigation, and exert mood or pressure through place-specific powers; room-weighted anomaly centers should tie to historical event geometry and prior room function
* support selective communication where a spirit communicates only to relevant actors or only through correctly framed questioning; ghost testimony may be emotionally sincere but factually slanted where the dead actor believes its own grievance while omitting self-incriminating truth
* support exact ritual protocol requirements including correct formula, language, and sanctioned funerary handling as distinct from generic anti-undead defeat; permanent disposal procedures such as burning or sanctified burial depth remain valid resolution requirements
* support root-event inheritance where institutional history (prior abuse programs, hidden experiments, abandonment patterns) defines the active anomaly method of a site rather than acting as lore-only backdrop; perpetrator work logs and unrecovered bodies are valid anchor surfaces
* distinguish baseline haunt-resolution semantics from multi-presence site-state logic (SPE-921) and building-scale purification execution (SPE-922); this layer provides the foundation those two issues build on
* connect the layer to undead, knowledge, ritual, and site-maintenance systems where relevant

Constraints:

* deterministic only
* no freeform ghost-therapy sandbox
* no assumption that combat defeat equals final retirement
* no assumption that remains discovery alone resolves all haunt cases
* no assumption that a ritual produces final safety or removes every presence indiscriminately once the expected formula is completed
* no assumption that every residual force stays fixed to one room or one remains-site when relational and object-bound anchors remain active
* prefer compact haunt-state and anchor-state packets over sprawling bespoke exorcism trees
* keep active anchors, resolution state, recurrence conditions, and post-destruction tracking legible enough for planning and debugging

Acceptance criteria:

* at least one haunt requires more than combat defeat to resolve permanently
* at least one case separates temporary disruption or banishment from final retirement
* at least one case remains unresolved until a non-remains anchor object or multi-part anchor condition is cleared
* at least one partial resolution recurs deterministically because a primary anchor condition remains active
* at least one case resolves through investigation-first or nonviolent closure logic instead of destruction-only routing
* at least one false-clear state persists after a ritual because a second presence or anchor remains unresolved
* at least one case routes withdrawal or nonoccupation as the correct resolution because direct neutralization is not viable during the active window
* at least one case derives its anchor and resolution path from the root-event method of the site rather than from the immediate manifest presence
* at least one case requires exact ritual protocol for final closure rather than ordinary defeat
* targeted tests cover deterministic diagnosis, anchor discovery, remains and object handling, recurrence checks, false-clear detection, withdrawal routing, and final closure state transitions

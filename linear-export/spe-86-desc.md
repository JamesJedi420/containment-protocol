Goal:
Implement urban service nodes as active operational packets so city access, support, and hidden functions depend on specific providers rather than generic menus.

Scope:

* define business and service nodes with operator identity, service families, stock or availability state, clientele profile, rumor output, and bounded local rules
* support legal-front versus hidden-function layering so shops, inns, shrines, clubs, and similar venues can conceal contraband, covert logistics, informant roles, detention space, or hidden caches; ordinary shops, taverns, and bazaar stalls may conceal magical goods, traps, guild ties, hidden treasure, covert patrons, or side-work rather than behaving as passive vendor endpoints
* support temporary rented capability, guided passage, specialist help, repair, knowledge brokerage, and similar service families that materially change access or risk
* support degraded, mixed-trust, or near-site service pockets that provide partial sanctuary, rumors, procurement, and compromised support under pressure
* support civic institutions, tolerated illicit quarters, structural black markets, and hostile-domain commerce where service access matters more than simple shop availability
* make hidden business functions, covert routing, and unstable operators legible enough to plan around
* support sewer and utility network traversal as a hidden traversal layer that lets anomalies and covert actors bypass street surveillance and domestic access assumptions; repeated-use movement through those networks can define impostor lairs through stored disguises, discarded forms, and accumulated personal effects rather than one isolated hideout clue
* support portal commerce and route surfaces embedded in mundane commercial thresholds including shop arches, counters, back rooms, and retail fixtures whose exit conditions are separately controlled; morally ambiguous but immediately useful merchants may provide gear, portal lore, or dangerous inventory while carrying hidden agenda or future-complication flags; missed key evidence should relocate to plausible custody holders rather than disappearing from the case
* support trust-gated testimony so local service nodes gate information by trust, spending, social class handling, and perceived threat; marginalized witnesses, neighborhood contacts, and specialist merchants may provide useful hints in the short term while still carrying hidden agendas, future hooks, or dangerous inventory not fully explained at point of sale; specialist archives and notebooks should remain access-gated handout surfaces reducing investigation friction when obtained through contract, theft, or favor
* support low-surveillance contact venues such as bathhouses, roadside shrines, and off-wall social spaces as valid rumor, handoff, and covert meeting hubs where patrol pressure is lower than ordinary city streets; escrowed mission funds or specialist supplies may require identity proof, password, faction reference, or leverage before release even when the holding contact was pre-briefed; guide contracts may exclude real combat support while still supplying route knowledge, local custom, and bounded translation or doctrine help
* support inn-class venues as full-service social hub packets combining lodging, food, drink, patrons, rumor flow, trustee functions, messaging, training access, and recurring operator behavior in one bounded venue; hospitality menus should carry identity and light utility through ordinary dishes, distinctive drinks, scarcity-preserved fare, and low-intensity timed effects such as scent masking, recovery support, or travel-comfort support; dominant clientele should remain a first-class venue variable shaping tone, service profile, rumor traffic, conflict risk, and security expectations; proprietor secrets and hidden venue state may radically reframe an inn once discovered without turning the site into a separate dungeon system
* support respectable crime fronts where ordinary taverns, warehouses, stores, and market stalls conceal logistics, fencing, hidden reserves, coded access, and political violence support behind ordinary civic commerce; distributed low-status vendors such as barbers, bards, and herbalists should remain useful intelligence nodes through repeated small disclosures; entertainment venues may shape rumor and public understanding actively through songs, performances, and public complaint loops; some hidden bases should invert by time-of-day, functioning as normal civic or commercial sites in daylight and politically explosive staging grounds at night
* support entertainment fronts concealing illegal job brokerage, gambling rooms, secret subvenues, insect-fighting pits, or comparable criminal side-markets behind ordinary leisure presentation; city establishments should remain differentiable through compact owner, quality, and specialty fields, with faction embedding making some businesses practical access nodes for smugglers, fences, pirates, exiles, or minority networks rather than neutral storefronts; temporary specialists recruited through city businesses or bounty offices may take percentage-share compensation rather than flat fees
* support clandestine venue layering with password entry, visible disarm screening, shallow searches that still allow concealed exceptions, segregated backstage space, protected cash or prize nodes, and covert exits; public festival or carnival layers should function as concealment cover for hostile action, covert observation, and witness unreliability rather than neutral atmosphere
* support dual-mode predatory hospitality where elite social spaces on the surface use hidden passages, twisting service routes, and off-axis rooms to isolate, charm, intoxicate, or stalk selected victims; social consumables may function simultaneously as status currency, concealment aid, and byproducts of local predation ecology rather than ordinary luxury goods
* support false refuges as hostile-site patterns where shelter, warmth, food, or apparent help conceal cages, remains, transformation victims, or predator infrastructure until close examination; environmental intake such as wrecks, storms, and washed-up survivors can function as a recurring supply chain feeding those hostile sites; false refuge should remain a valid hospitality inversion pattern in both isolated rural environments and urban vice fronts
* support service nodes that hide predatory or exploitative functions beneath the surface of ordinary care, warmth, food, shelter, or intake; medical or intake data may be repurposed as an eligibility screen for exploiters targeting healthy, low-search-risk subjects rather than functioning as care infrastructure only
* support domestic decorative objects as hidden occult anchors whose threat state remains invisible until activation; ordinary residential art, memorials, or gallery overflow may function as necromantic anchors, and crowded social gatherings inside the same dwelling can amplify the threat by blocking private reconciliation, obscuring early warning cues, and converting the home into a dense siege point once the anchor activates
* support hospitality fronts that invite entrants into apparently safe employment, shelter, or elite access only to convert the visit into a containment trap through sealed rooms, hidden wards, or controlled staff procedure; some sites may secretly function as inn, ferry house, warehouse, contraband cellar, tunnel hub, and hidden river-exit site simultaneously; ordinary room furnishings such as beds, podiums, and counters may conceal vertical routes or narrow storage shafts; everyday architectural fixtures such as fireplaces may hide dungeon ingress activated by mechanical or magical triggers
* support in-site commerce inside danger zones where player-run or third-party microbusinesses operate directly in hostile spaces creating shelter, rumor flow, customer interaction, and conflict; rival operator groups may run parallel commerce loops with their own stock, policy, and hidden reserves inside the same site
* support mark-location taxonomy for heists derived from site class: customs houses, guild halls, warehouses, residences, sacred sites, municipal buildings, ruins, markets, farms, cult sites, and criminal fronts each carry different default access patterns, defenses, and fallout; sacred institutions and elite residences should remain especially distinct heist targets because their protection mixes legal, social, domestic, and sometimes metaphysical defense layers
* support social-front surveillance architecture and escape geometry so inns, clubs, or hospitality fronts may simultaneously host public traffic, private negotiation, acoustic eavesdropping channels, hidden storage, secret basement links, and operator escape routes; on-demand architectural conversion such as trap doors, ramp transformations, or similar local geometry shifts should remain valid venue-defense tools when owned by the operator
* support disguised logistics houses and shrine-front transport nodes where innocuous religious or civic buildings hide long-range logistics capability, rare-goods routing, and strategic transport functions behind an ordinary public facade
* support mining-town and extraction-settlement service ecosystems exposing dense networks of rope makers, wagon support, inns, ferries, temples, offices, smiths, animal breeders, barrel makers, lamp makers, and similar specialized utility nodes; hireling encounters may package immediate equipment, bounded regional lore, and temporary practical support together rather than acting as pure combat reinforcements
* support layered NPC-embedded rumors where service interactions surface hidden leads, contradictions, and objective hooks organically; xenophobic settlements may still preserve narrow profitable lanes for outsider commerce through guarded guilds, harbor contacts, or sanctioned brokers without becoming broadly welcoming; informal sellers and back-channel traders may offer claimed anomaly items whose authenticity must be verified separately from the sale claim
* support underworld economies as coherent service families including fences, dues, cuts, laundering, specialist hires, and black-market movement; many covert tools should remain restricted by shadow-market access, faction contacts, or local illegality rather than universally purchasable; hidden businesses should function as access brokers for contraband, rumor, false goods, and covert logistics
* support gray-market hubs and protected foreign-contact enclaves in hostile or monstrous polities where stolen goods, luxuries, services, intelligence, and morally compromised trade can transact under bounded rules; exclusionary realms may preserve narrow foreign-contact enclaves for trade, diplomacy, or tolerated exchange without becoming broadly welcoming
* support rank-shaped repair quality where repair outcomes depend on item class, repairer status, and whether the work is rushed, crude, or skilled; higher social standing may improve access to better repair outcomes without making repair purely a money check; venue-dependent contamination risk should vary by venue cleanliness rather than being universally safe once purchased in a city; some trade goods may secretly carry anomalous or hidden-effect payloads making appraisal a question of function as well as price; low-status contacts such as beggars can function as rumor and prediction markets with variable payment floors, topic breadth, and uncertain truthfulness
* support hidden-cache ecology where ordinary trades routinely maintain concealed reserves, buried money, hidden documents, contraband, or private support stores behind normal workspaces; professions should act as distinct rumor and intelligence nodes surfacing nearby raids, political stress, supply shortages, or covert loyalties
* support district encounter cadence varying by district or route class with different cadence on main thoroughfares, back streets, and public interiors; patrol presence may control riot or property damage without making the city safe for outsiders; street factions can swing between predation, negotiation, and short-term cooperation based on local contact outcome; logistics spaces may mostly yield low-value stock while rarely hiding disproportionately useful disguise, navigation, or infiltration tools
* support paid neutral ferrymen and service actors with volatile disposition that can degrade into hostility through repetition, provocation, or stress; temporary allied support groups may join through explicit payment and loot-share or outcome-share terms rather than alignment alone; these arrangements should remain bounded service contracts with clear support expectations, not permanent recruitment by default
* support venue-local rule enforcement such as dress requirements, confiscation, expulsion, or house-specific penalties that can alter service access and local risk without escalating to full civic arrest automatically

Constraints:

* deterministic only
* no full merchant-management game
* no simulation of every civilian in the city
* no assumption that patrol presence equals civilian safety for outsiders
* no assumption that hostile or monstrous polities are trade-inert violence zones
* prefer compact service-node packets and reusable service families over bespoke per-shop minigames
* keep urban services operationally meaningful without turning the hub into open-ended sandbox shopping
* hidden business functions and covert layers should remain legible enough for mission planning and debugging rather than becoming opaque prose flavor

Acceptance criteria:

* at least one service node exposes operator identity, local availability, clientele behavior, and rumor output in one reusable packet
* at least one storefront or institution hides a meaningful secondary function
* at least one service family provides temporary capability, route access, or bounded support rather than permanent ownership only
* at least one service outcome varies by trust, availability, access fit, or local risk terms
* at least one venue uses day-night role inversion, clandestine layering, or predatory hospitality dual-mode behavior
* at least one inn-class venue operates as a social hub packet with rumor flow, trustee functions, and operator-specific behavior rather than a generic rest node
* at least one false refuge presents as safe before revealing hostile-site structure
* at least one mark-location type carries a distinct heist access pattern and defense profile
* at least one district or route class uses non-uniform encounter cadence and nonprotective patrol semantics
* at least one gray-market hub or protected foreign-contact enclave functions in a hostile polity
* targeted tests cover node generation, hidden-function reveal, trust-gated access, temporary-service acquisition, clandestine-layer traversal, and downstream integration
